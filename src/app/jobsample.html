      Your Role and Responsibilities
      Thank you for your interest.
      This role is located ONSITE (after covid) Southbury, CT or NYC, NY.
      There is no relocation offered and no remote option (post covid).

      Do you love the challenge of keeping enterprise assets secure? Do you want every day to be a bit different and allow you to work with individuals across the spectrum of the organization? If so, please read on.

      The Manager of CIO Vulnerability Management position is responsible for helping to safeguard the company's assets, intellectual property, and computer systems in support of the CIO’s business objectives. The applicant will be responsible for detecting vulnerabilities, researching potential impact to the organization, and communicating the risks to IBM’s business units. The CIO Vulnerability Management Manager prioritizes work among a team of skilled analysts to address complex or difficult problems as needed within the CIO Cybersecurity Operations Center environment.

      This role requires a passion for researching, finding, and identifying the latest cyber threats to IBM. Using a multitude of sources, both internal and external to IBM, the manager of vulnerability management will work to develop proactive, anticipatory, and reactionary actions against aggressors.

      Job Duties
      • Provide leadership, governance and oversight to the effectiveness of the Vulnerability Management program within the organization
      • Proactively measure the effectiveness of the program through monitoring conformance against baseline, patching and application security requirements and standards
      • Analyze and prioritize scan results and coordinate with CIO Cybersecurity staff to assist in identifying mitigation strategies
      • Interface and coordinate with system owners and support teams to analyze and review mitigation strategies; provide guidance and assist when strategies need to be enhanced
      • Coordinate scanning for CIO assets, report discovered vulnerabilities, and assist with mitigation strategies for vulnerabilities that cannot be corrected
      • Create and maintain a strategic reporting mechanism to ensure stakeholders understand Objectives &Key Results (OKRs) associated with the vulnerability management program
      • Manage a team of Cybersecurity analysts as part of the Vulnerability Management program in the CIO
      • Use your strong communication and advocacy skills, both verbal and written, with the ability to express complex and technical issues in clear and concise business terms
      • As a self-starter focused on learning and continuous improvement bring your passion for cybersecurity and stay on top of security, threats, and technology trends and make a difference.
      CIODEM20

      Required Technical and Professional Expertise
      • Minimum of 5 plus years of Information Security / Cybersecurity environment experience
      • Advanced Experience with industry vulnerability scanning and assessment tools
      • Advanced Experience in gathering, assessing and distributing threat intelligence
      • Experience in performing verification and validation activities
      • Experience in performing security configuration compliance verification and gap assessments

      Preferred Technical And Professional Expertise
      • 2 plus years working in Information Security Technology or Vulnerability Management or related discipline
      • Experience with MITRE ATT&CK framework
      • Experience with threat hunting and threat intelligence
      • Experience with industry standard defensive tools including EDR
      • Comfortable working with a team in an agile environment
